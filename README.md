📊 HR Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

🚀 Project Overview

This project is an interactive HR Analytics dashboard built using Power BI to analyze employee attrition.

The goal of this project is to answer key business questions:

- Who is leaving the company?
- Why are employees leaving?
- When are employees leaving?

The dashboard is designed with a multi-page structure and includes advanced Power BI features for
better data exploration and decision-making.


🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

- Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)

📂 Dataset

The dataset contains employee-level HR data, including:

- Age & Age Group
- Department & Job Role
- Salary (Monthly Income & Salary Slab)
- Years at Company
- Attrition (Yes/No)
- Performance & Satisfaction metrics


📈 Dashboard Pages

1️⃣ Executive Overview

"Executive Overview" (screenshots/executive_overview.png)

- Displays high-level KPIs:
  - Total Employees
  - Attrition Count
  - Attrition Rate
  - Active Employees
- Workforce distribution by department
- Job roles with highest attrition
- Attrition trend over years
- Attrition split (Yes vs No)

2️⃣ Department Analysis

"Department Analysis" (screenshots/department_analysis.png)

- KPIs based on selected filters:
  - Attrition Count
  - Attrition Rate
  - Average Experience
- Attrition by Job Role (with Top 5 vs All toggle using bookmarks)
- Matrix view with:
  - Total Employees
  - Attrition Count
  - Attrition Rate (with conditional formatting)
- Slicers:
  - Department
  - Salary Slab
  - Age Group
- Clear slicers button for better user experience


3️⃣ Attrition Driver Analysis

"Attrition Drivers" (screenshots/attrition_driver.png)

- Identifies key drivers of attrition:
  - Attrition by Age Group (Who is leaving)
  - Attrition vs Monthly Income (Why they are leaving)
  - Attrition by Years at Company (When they leave)
  - Heatmap (Job Role vs Department)

---

4️⃣ Drill-through Page

"Drillthrough" (screenshots/drillthrough.png)

- Provides detailed employee-level data
- Triggered from Job Role selection
- Helps in deep analysis of specific segments


 Key Features

- Interactive slicers and filters
- KPI indicators for business monitoring
- Bookmark toggle (Top 5 vs All job roles)
- Drill-through functionality for detailed insights
- Custom tooltip page for additional context
- Conditional formatting to highlight high attrition areas
- Multi-page navigation for structured analysis


💡 Key Insights

- Sales Executive and Laboratory Technician roles show higher attrition
- Employees aged 26–35 contribute the most to attrition
- Attrition is higher during early years of employment
- Mid salary range employees show higher exit rates


📌 Conclusion

This dashboard helps organizations:

- Understand employee attrition patterns
- Identify high-risk departments and roles
- Take data-driven actions to improve retention
